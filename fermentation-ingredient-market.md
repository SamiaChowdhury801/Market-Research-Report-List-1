<p><ul><li>Batch</li><li>Continuous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fermentation ingredient market can be categorized into two main types: batch and continuous systems. Batch fermentation involves processing fixed volumes of ingredients in discrete batches, allowing for flexibility and control over specific conditions. This method is often used for specialty products. In contrast, continuous fermentation maintains a constant flow of raw materials, resulting in a steady output of products. This approach is typically more efficient and scalable, suitable for high-demand products, ensuring consistent quality and reduced processing time.</p></p>
