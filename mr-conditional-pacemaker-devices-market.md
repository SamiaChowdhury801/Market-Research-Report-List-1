<p><p>The MR Conditional Pacemaker Devices market consists of two main types: external and implantable devices. External devices are used temporarily to manage heart rhythms and can be removed after stabilization. They are typically utilized in emergency scenarios or for short-term needs. Implantable devices, on the other hand, are surgically placed within the body for long-term heart rhythm management. Both types are designed to be compatible with magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) procedures, allowing patients to undergo imaging safely.</p></p>
