<p><ul><li>Hollow Glass</li><li>Vacuum Glass</li><li>Laminated Glass</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The architectural acoustic insulation glass market includes several types designed to enhance soundproofing. Hollow glass features an air gap between panes, reducing sound transmission. Vacuum glass employs a vacuum seal, providing superior insulation and noise reduction. Laminated glass consists of two glass layers bonded with an acoustic interlayer, effectively dampening vibrations and minimizing noise. Each type serves specific architectural needs, balancing aesthetics with sound insulation, making them essential in urban environments for improved comfort and privacy.</p></p>
