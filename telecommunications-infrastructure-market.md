<p><ul><li>Lattice Tower</li><li>Guyed Tower</li><li>Monopole Tower</li><li>Stealth Tower</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The telecommunications infrastructure market comprises various tower types essential for wireless communication. Lattice towers, known for their triangular structure, offer high elevation and support multiple antennas. Guyed towers rely on tensioned cables for stability, making them suitable for taller installations. Monopole towers are single, slender structures that minimize land usage while supporting communications equipment. Stealth towers blend into the environment, mimicking natural structures to reduce visual impact. Other markets may include small cells and distributed antenna systems, enhancing network capacity and coverage.</p></p>
