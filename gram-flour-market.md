<p><p>The Gram Flour market can be divided into two main types: Desi and Kabuli. Desi gram flour is made from small, dark chickpeas, offering a robust flavor and higher nutritional value, often used in traditional Indian dishes. Kabuli gram flour, derived from larger, lighter-colored chickpeas, has a milder taste and is preferred for its finer texture, commonly used in Middle Eastern cuisines. Both types cater to diverse culinary preferences and dietary needs, influencing their market demand and pricing.</p></p>
