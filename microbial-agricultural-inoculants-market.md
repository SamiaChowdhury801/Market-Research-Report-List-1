<p><p>The microbial agricultural inoculants market consists of two primary types: soil inoculation and seed inoculation. Soil inoculation involves applying beneficial microorganisms directly to the soil to enhance nutrient availability, improve soil structure, and promote plant growth. Seed inoculation, on the other hand, involves treating seeds with microorganisms before planting to boost germination rates, increase resistance to diseases, and improve overall plant health. Both methods aim to optimize agricultural productivity and sustainability through the integration of beneficial microbes.</p></p>
