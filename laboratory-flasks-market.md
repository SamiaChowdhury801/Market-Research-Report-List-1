<p><p>The laboratory flasks market encompasses various types designed for specific purposes. Conical flasks are ideal for mixing and swirling liquids, while round-bottom flasks offer even heating and are suitable for boiling. Florence flasks, with their round shape and narrow neck, are used for heating liquids. Volumetric flasks are precision tools for measuring volumes accurately. Büchner flasks, characterized by a flat bottom and sidearm, facilitate vacuum filtration. Other types include Erlenmeyer flasks, test tubes, and culture flasks, catering to diverse laboratory needs.</p></p>
