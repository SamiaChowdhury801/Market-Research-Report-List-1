<p><p>The open-loop current sensor market is broadly categorized into two types: isolated and non-isolated sensors. Isolated sensors provide electrical separation between the sensor and the circuit, enhancing safety and protecting against voltage spikes. They are ideal for high-voltage applications. In contrast, non-isolated sensors lack this separation, making them simpler and more cost-effective for low-voltage applications. Both types are vital in various industries, including automotive, industrial automation, and renewable energy, catering to different safety and functionality requirements.</p></p>
