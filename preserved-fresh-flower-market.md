<p><p>The preserved fresh flower market comprises various drying methods that enhance longevity and aesthetic appeal. Air drying is a traditional technique where flowers are hung upside down in a cool, dark environment. Freeze drying involves removing moisture through sublimation, preserving color and structure effectively. Silica gel drying utilizes silica particles to absorb moisture quickly, maintaining the flowers' original shapes and colors. Other methods include glycerin preservation and resin encapsulation, offering diverse options for consumers seeking durable floral arrangements.</p></p>
