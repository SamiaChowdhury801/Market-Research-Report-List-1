<p><p>The robotic-assisted hip replacement market encompasses three main types: passive systems, semi-active systems, and active systems. Passive systems provide surgeons with enhanced visualization and precision through robotic guidance but require manual control. Semi-active systems combine robotic guidance with surgeon input, allowing for some automated adjustments during surgery. Active systems fully automate the surgical process, enabling the robot to independently execute tasks based on pre-operative planning. These distinctions highlight varying levels of surgeon assistance and automation within the robotic surgical landscape.</p></p>
