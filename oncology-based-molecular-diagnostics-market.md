<p><p>Oncology-based molecular diagnostics encompass various techniques for detecting cancer-related genetic alterations. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA sequences, facilitating the identification of mutations. Multiplex PCR allows simultaneous testing of multiple targets, enhancing efficiency. In Situ Hybridization visualizes specific nucleic acid sequences within cells, aiding in tissue analysis. Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification Technology (INAAT) allows for nucleic acid amplification at a constant temperature, offering rapid results without complex equipment. Together, these methods advance precision in cancer diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>
